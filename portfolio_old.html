Welcome to my UX portfolio. Here you'll find examples of systems I've designed and built for academic research for various topics in human-computer interaction (HCI), including creative collaboration with large language models (LLMs), remote guidance for physical activity via ungrounded haptics, using instructional tools in makerspaces, and performing collaborative tasks with robots. You'll also see examples of my commercial designs for real-time collaboration support.

User research

Prior research in the field of HCI has shown that writers, especially creative writers, are reluctant to accept intelligent writing support tools due to concerns about ownership and artistic integrity. I conducted interviews with professional and hobbyist fiction writers to outline avenues for writing support that fiction writers would want to use. I focused my questions on high-level activities in storytelling to understand the practices authors use to shape their stories, and the challenges they encounter within those practices. I identified four conceptual formats for writing support based on the interview responses. A video mock-up of each concept can be seen below.
